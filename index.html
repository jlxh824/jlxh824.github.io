Research

The following are descriptions of research projects I have done.

  • On the strong continuity and local equicontinuity of Ornstein-Uhlenbeck semigroups in open sets of Hilbert spaces. This was my honours thesis. For an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process in a separable Hilbert space, it was shown in a 2001 paper that the associated transition semigroup is a C0-semigroup in the space Cb(H) endowed with the mixed topology. I proved an extension of this result to the case of the killed semigroup in an open set of H.

  • Weak convergence of metric gradient descent for strictly convex objectives on uniformly convex Banach spaces. In this paper, I introduce the metric gradient descent algorithm and prove that it converges weakly when optimising strictly convex functionals on a uniformly convex Banach space under some additional continuity assumptions. This was written while I was a Summer Research Intern at UQ during the 2020/21 summer.

Expository Work

  • Representation theory of compact groups. We develop the analysis on compact groups up to the Peter-Weyl theorem.

  • Riesz representation theorem for non-compact spaces. We introduce the mixed topology on spaces of continuous functions and shows that if X is a topological space in which continuous functions separates points, then the dual of the space of bounded continuous functions on X equipped with the mixed topology is the space of regular signed Borel measures on X.

  • Characterisation of reflexivity for spaces with an unconditional basis. I give a proof that a Banach space with an unconditional basis is reflexive if and only if it does not contain a closed subspace isomorphic to c0 or ℓ1. The content of this document is my own solution to a difficult assignment problem.

  • The Johnson-Linderstrauss lemma. This short essay was written for MATH3325 (Semester 2 2020) at ANU. I give an overview of the proof of the Johnson-Lindenstrauss lemma using concentration of measure in Gauss space and describe an alternative approach using Haar measure on the Grassmannian.

  • Beyond martingales. This was a term paper written for MATH3015 (Semester 2 2020) at ANU. I give an overview of the construction of the stochastic integral with respect to a semimartingale.
