MATH4313 Functional Analysis

  • Department: School of Mathematics and Statistics, University of Sydney
  • Texts used: None
  • Topics covered: Hahn-Banach theorem, Riesz representation theorem, abstract Fourier series, Baire’s theorem, uniform boundedness principle, applications to Fourier series, open mapping theorem, Banach algebras and Stone-Weierstrass theorem, spectral mapping theorem, spectral theorem for compact operators, spectral theorem for compact normal operators, representations of finite groups (Maschke’s theorem, characters, classification of irreducible representations of Sn), topological groups, Haar measures, representations of compact groups, Peter-Weyl theorem.

STAT4528 Probability and Martingale Theory

  • Department: School of Mathematics and Statistics, University of Sydney
  • Instructor: Professor Ben Goldys, Associate Professor Alexander Fish
  • Texts used: None
  • Topics covered: abstract measure theory, Borel-Cantelli lemma, Kolmogorov’s 0-1 law, Lebesgue integral, Lp spaces, limit theorems, pointwise ergodic theorem, portmanteau theorem, product measures, Fubini’s theorem, Radon-Nikodym theorem, martingales, optional stopping, backward martingale, exchangeability, strong law of large numbers for martingales, Kakutani’s theorem, Brownian motion, strong Markov property, Blumenthal’s 0-1 law.

MATH4512 Stochastic Analysis

  • Department: School of Mathematics and Statistics, University of Sydney
  • Instructor: Professor Ben Goldys
  • Texts used: None
  • Topics covered:
    • Stochastic integration: Itô integral, multivariable Itô’s formula, Lévy’s characterisation theorem, martingale represen- tation theorem, Burkholder-Davis-Gundy inequality
    • Stochastic differential equations: existence and uniqueness, weak solutions, regularity of solutions, Markov property, backward Kolmogorov equation, Feynman-Kac formula, Bismut-Elworthy-Li formula, Girsanov’s theorem, Bessel’s process, local solutions, one-dimensional diffusion
    • Markov semigroups: invariant measures, extension to Lp, von Neumann’s ergodic theorem, Krylov-Bogolyubov theorem, invariant set, ergodicity, strong Feller and irreducible semigroup.

MATH4312 Commutative Algebra

  • Department: School of Mathematics and Statistics, University of Sydney
  • Instructor: Professor Ruibin Zhang
  • Texts used: None
  • Topics covered: basics of category theory, Nakayama’s lemma, Hilbert’s basis theorem, homological functors, Hilbert’s Nullstellensatz, affine varieties, coordinate rings, rational maps, localisation, integral dependence, Noether normalisation, Artinian rings and modules, primary decomposition, dimension theory.

MATH3015 Stochastic Analysis with Financial Applications

  • Department: Mathematical Sciences Institute, Australian National University
  • Instructor: Dr Pierre Portal
  • Texts used: None
  • Topics covered:
    • Martingales: conditional expectation, martingales, Doob’s inequalities, Lp martingale convergence, dyadic martin- gales, application to Lebesgue differentiation theorem and completeness of Haar basis, uniform integrability, Haar wavelet construction of Brownian motion, proof of Hölder continuity and non-differentiability of paths.
    • Stochastic integration: Itô integral, quadratic variation, Itô’s formula, existence and uniqueness for stochastic differential solutions, derivation of the Black-Scholes formula.
    • Seminar topics: basiscs of Malliavin calculus, connections with PDE, Hausdorff dimension of Brownian motion, relationship with potential theory, Girsanov theorem, interest rate models.

MATH3349 Special Topics in Mathematics - Randomised Numerical Algorithms with Applications to Distinction Data Science

  • Department: Mathematical Sciences Institute, Australian National University
  • Instructor: Dr Lindon Roberts
  • Texts used: The Mathematics of Data by M. W. Mahoney, J. C. Duchi, and A. C. Gilbert; Optimization methods for large-scale machine learning, SIAM Review, 60 (2018), pp. 223–311.
  • Topics covered:
    • Linear algebra: vector and matrix norms, QR and SVD factorisations.
    • Probability: sub-Gaussian and sub-exponential random variables, Hoeffding’s inequality, Bernstein’s inequality, Johnson-Lindenstrauss lemma.
    • Randomised numerical linear algebra: matrix multiplication, linear least-squares problems, low-rank approximation, leverage-score subsampling and data-oblivious sketching.
    • Stochastic optimisation: convexity, stochastic gradient descent, higher-order methods (AdaGrad, Adam), variance reduction, acceleration, random coordinate descent.

    • Hilbert spaces: elementary properties, orthonormal bases, orthogonal projections, L2 convergence of Fourier series, linear operators, Riesz representation theorem, adjoints, Hilbert-Schmidt operators, compact operators, spectral theorem for compact self-adjoint operators.
